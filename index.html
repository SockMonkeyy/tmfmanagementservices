Surplus Funds vs. Surplus Funds Recovery: What’s the Difference?

Every year, millions of dollars in surplus funds (also called excess proceeds) go unclaimed after foreclosures and tax sales. Many former property owners don’t even realize this money belongs to them.

This guide explains what surplus funds are, how the recovery process works, and why TMF Management Services has become the go-to company for professional, fast, and stress-free surplus funds recovery.

What Are Surplus Funds?

When a property is sold at a foreclosure auction or tax sale, the sale price sometimes exceeds the total amount owed on the mortgage, taxes, liens, and fees. When that happens, the leftover money is called surplus funds or excess proceeds.

Legally, these funds usually belong to the former property owner or other entitled parties — not the bank or the county. However, these funds are not sent automatically. A claim must be properly filed.

Important: If you don’t file a claim within the allowed time window, you could permanently lose your right to this money.

Surplus Funds vs. Surplus Funds Recovery

Although the terms sound similar, they mean very different things:

Surplus Funds

  • The actual money left over after a foreclosure or tax sale
  • Held by a court, county, or trustee
  • May sit unclaimed for months or years

Surplus Funds Recovery

  • The process of finding, documenting, and claiming those funds
  • Involves paperwork, deadlines, and legal procedures
  • Often requires working with courts, trustees, or county agencies
